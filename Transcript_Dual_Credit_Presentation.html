A freshman applicant is a current high school student (with or without college credit) or a high school graduate with no college credit earned after high school graduation. reference: TAMU

  • You may apply as a freshman as long as your college coursework is designated as dual-credit.
  • Dual-credit college credits transfer after you are accepted as a freshman.
  • Once you take your first college course after high school graduation, you must apply as a transfer student.

Dual Credit Coursework significantly reduces the number of time you are in school to meet your educational goals and the amount of money you have to pay.

  • Do you really want to take the same class twice?
  • Entry level classes at a junior college are often far smaller than those at a large university and not considered "weed-out" classes.
  • Compare the price tag between a year of dual credit coursework and state university after high school graduation
    • TAMU
    • Dual-credit classes are significantly cheaper than local homeschool classes.
    • From the Houston Community College website... "The Houston City College Board of Trustees has waived tuition and fees for dual credit students. Students living within taxing districts (In-District) may take dual credit classes at no charge. Students living outside of the taxing district (Out-of-District) do not pay tuition, but they do pay tuition out-of-district fees."
    • Retaking a dual-credit class is not as financially painful as retaking a class at a university.

Graduating high school with an associates degree has many benefits:

  • Qualifies you for a job with higher pay and benefits faster. This can be big.
  • Helps to protect credits from having to be repeated.
  • If the right courses were selected, all the credits will count towards a future degree reducing the time and cost of school.
